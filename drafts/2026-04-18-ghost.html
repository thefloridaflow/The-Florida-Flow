⛑️ Safety Tip

Long-period swells are deceptive. Today's Space Coast buoy shows 2.3 ft seas at a 9-second period. That sounds modest, but long-period swells travel deep in the water column and can catch divers and swimmers off guard with sudden surges and surge at depth, reduced visibility from sediment disturbance, and unexpected bottom pull. Treat 9-second swells with the same respect you would larger short-period chop.

Plan for Monday now. The approaching front brings 18 kt winds and 8.3mm of rain. If you have gear to prep, a boat to secure, or a dive trip to reschedule, do it today or tomorrow morning. Monday is not a day to test the water.
