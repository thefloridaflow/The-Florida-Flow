The Briefing

A quiet Friday with light winds and normal South Florida seas

Observed buoys this morning tell a relaxed story. Seas are running 3.0 ft at 8-second periods off the Treasure Coast (buoy 41114, 6.5 nm offshore, water 76.3 F) and 3.0 ft off the Space Coast (buoy 41009, 20 nm offshore, winds 9.7 kt SSE). Farther south, buoy 41122 at 23 nm offshore shows 2.3 ft at a short 4-second period for the Deerfield to Miami corridor. The short period on that southern buoy means surface chop with less disturbance to the bottom — a positive note for visibility south of Palm Beach.

The Gulf Stream west wall sits close: 3 nm east-northeast of Lake Worth and 10 nm northeast of Port Everglades as of April 20. No Gulf Stream hazards are noted. Winds this evening stay under 10 kt through 11 PM. Saturday looks slightly calmer still, with max gusts dropping to 10 kt.

Blue Heron Bridge has two windows today. The afternoon window at 3:17 PM (low tide 2.4 ft, optimal quality, 2:47 to 3:47 PM) is the one to target. Wind at LKWF1 is 8.9 kt SE — well within the comfortable range for BHB. Rainbow Reef (Key Largo) logged 60 ft visibility Thursday afternoon with 2 to 3 ft waves and an east wind of 15 kt. Conditions today are lighter than Thursday, so visibility is expected to hold well.
