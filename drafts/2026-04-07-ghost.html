Safety Tip

Short Period Seas are Rough Seas

Today's 3.3 ft seas off SE Florida are arriving at a 4-second period, which means waves are stacked close together and coming fast. Short-period wind chop is often more physically demanding than taller swell with longer periods. Boats will pitch and roll more aggressively, and divers doing giant stride entries off a pitching vessel face elevated risk of gear contact and disorientation. The 22-knot NNE wind at Blue Heron Bridge also means surface conditions will feel brisker than the tidal flat suggests. If you are paddling, diving from a boat, or launching in the Palm Beach zone today, verify live surface conditions with your operator or divemaster before committing.
