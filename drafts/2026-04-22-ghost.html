The Briefing

An active SCA clears by 2 PM — afternoon and evening look workable

A Small Craft Advisory runs through 2 PM EDT today, and the buoys back it up. The Treasure Coast buoy (41114, just 6.5 nm offshore) is reading 5.2 ft at an 8-second period, and NWS is calling 5-7 ft seas occasionally to 9 ft offshore this morning with E winds 15-20 kt. That is genuinely rough by any standard. The Gulf Stream is running especially hazardous, with 20-25 kt winds and 7-10 ft seas reported through this morning. If you had morning offshore plans, those need to move.

The good news is real: NWS expects seas to drop to 3-4 ft tonight with winds easing to around 15 kt with gusts to 20 kt. The Open-Meteo hourly data confirms the trend, with gusts dropping from 22 kt this afternoon toward 18 kt by 8 PM. Blue Heron Bridge looks like the clear winner today. Wind at LKWF1 is 18.1 kt right now, which is borderline for a BHB dive, but the optimal afternoon window (12:29-1:29 PM) arrives right as the SCA lifts and winds ease. Narcosis Dive reported 60 ft of visibility and 76°F water two days ago with just 2-3 ft waves — that kind of clarity could return tonight and Thursday as seas settle.

Looking ahead, Thursday and Friday bring lighter conditions: winds dropping to 11-12 kt and seas coming down, though some drizzle is expected through the weekend. Saturday and Sunday stay partly cloudy with modest swell. This week does not bring a prolonged calm, but the window from Thursday onward looks considerably more accessible than today's morning.

Activity planner

Wednesday · 6 activities
Scuba Diving Wait/Afternoon

Offshore boats face 5-7 ft seas and SCA conditions through 2 PM. Afternoon looks better as seas drop to 3-4 ft. BHB is the best option today, especially the 12:29-1:29 PM window as the SCA lifts. Wind at 18 kt this morning is borderline for BHB entry. Verify with your operator before booking.

Surfing Blown Out

E winds at 15-20 kt are onshore and well above the 15 kt blown-out threshold. Swell is there (4.6-5.2 ft) but the onshore chop destroys any shape. Conditions improve tomorrow as winds ease below 12 kt and seas settle. Verify with local shops for current break conditions.

Kayak / SUP Not Recommended

Wind at 18 kt E and seas 4.6-5.2 ft put open-water kayaking and SUP firmly in the risk zone today. Intracoastal waterways have moderate chop per NWS. Sheltered mangrove paddling may be manageable, but exposed water is not suitable. Reassess Thursday when winds drop to 11 kt.

Boating / Fishing SCA: Stay Inshore

Small Craft Advisory in effect until 2 PM. Offshore is not recommended for smaller vessels. Seas of 5-7 ft occasionally to 9 ft are dangerous for boats under approximately 26 ft. Inshore and ICW fishing is generally fine. Large offshore vessels may resume this afternoon. Confirm with your captain.

Beach Caution

Expect red flag conditions this morning with active swell and strong onshore winds. Rip current risk is elevated with 4.6-5.2 ft seas at 8-second periods and shore-normal E/ENE direction. The beach itself is fine for walking. Swimming is risky this morning. Afternoon may ease. Swim near a lifeguard stand and check posted flags on arrival.

Gulf Stream Do Not Go

NWS is calling 20-25 kt winds and 7-10 ft seas in the Gulf Stream through this morning. The west wall sits 3 nm east-northeast of Lake Worth and 10 nm northeast of Port Everglades today. Hazardous conditions. Do not attempt Gulf Stream crossings or trolling runs today. Reassess Friday or Saturday as conditions settle.

Beach report

Casual beachgoers · plain English

Water temperatures are running 75-79°F depending on where you are along the coast, which is comfortable for most swimmers. The sea state this morning is rough, with waves in the 4-5 ft range and a stiff easterly breeze driving steady onshore chop.

Rip current risk is high this morning. Seas are running 4.6-5.2 ft at an 8-second period from the east-northeast, which is exactly the setup that generates strong rips along the Florida coast. Swim near a lifeguard stand. If you get caught in a rip, swim parallel to shore, not against the current.

Expect red flags this morning. Conditions may ease toward yellow this afternoon as the advisory lifts and seas begin to drop. UV is rated High today at index 6, climbing to 8 tomorrow, so sunscreen is important regardless of clouds. The afternoon window after 2 PM is the better time to visit if the beach is your goal today.

Conditions change — always check posted flags and swim near a lifeguard.
