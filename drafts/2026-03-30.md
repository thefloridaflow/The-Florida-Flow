# East Wind Blowout from Cape to Lauderdale — Keys Holding, BHB Window at Sunrise

*Monday, March 30, 2026 · The Florida Flow*

---

## Conditions at a Glance

A persistent east swell is hammering everything from Cape Canaveral down to Fort Lauderdale — Small Craft Advisory in effect, 6–10 ft seas, east winds 10–25 kts depending on your latitude. The Florida Keys are the exception: northeast winds backing off to near 10 kts, seas 2–3 ft. Blue Heron Bridge has a window today but you'll need to be in the water at sunrise — high tide hits at 7:15 AM, right as the park opens.

Blown out. Gulf Stream still running but nobody wants to be on the surface in 20–25 kt easterlies. Shore diving at BHB only — and the window is razor-thin.

Rough. East winds 20–25 kts means most operators are pulling the plug on offshore trips. Check with your charter before you drive.

**Florida Keys** — 2–3 ft · 5 s · NE 10–15 kts (decreasing) · ~75°F
Best conditions in the state right now. Hawk Channel especially clean — winds dropping, nearshore light chop to smooth. If you can get south, go south.
