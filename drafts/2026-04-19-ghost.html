✅ Calm seas and light winds today. BHB morning window opens 9:44 AM with 50-60 ft visibility confirmed by operators. Monday brings rain and the week's strongest winds, so today and Tuesday are your best shots on the water.

Buoys are showing calm to mild conditions coast-wide this morning. Buoy 41009, sitting 20 nautical miles off the Space Coast, is reading 2.3 ft seas at an 8-second period with an 11.7-knot south wind. That long 8-second period means these swells are traveling some distance and will reach the bottom in shallower water, which can knock visibility down a tier compared to short choppy wind swell. Buoy 41114, 6.5 nautical miles off the Treasure Coast near Vero and Fort Pierce, also shows 2.3 ft at 8 seconds with water at 78.8°F. Down south, buoy 41122, roughly 23 nautical miles offshore covering Deerfield through Miami, is reading just 1.3 ft at a short 3-second period, which barely affects the bottom. Key West area buoy 42095 is at 1.6 ft, 5 seconds. Blue Heron Bridge and Palm Beach show 11.1 knots ESE from buoy LKWF1, with water at 79.9°F.

The NWS marine forecast for Jupiter Inlet to Deerfield Beach calls for south winds 5 to 10 knots through tonight, seas remaining under 2 feet, and light chop on the Intracoastal. The bigger story is Monday: rain showers, winds up to 18 knots with gusts to 23 knots, and 8.3 mm of rain in the Open-Meteo data. The NWS synopsis confirms a cold front passage is expected to bring hazardous conditions as early as Tuesday, with seas and winds increasing in the wake of that front. Tuesday and Wednesday will need watching. For now, today is the clean window: light southerly flow, seas under 2 ft nearshore, and operator-confirmed visibility of 50 to 60 feet in West Palm Beach as of yesterday.

Blue Heron Bridge has two tidal windows today. The morning optimal window runs 9:44 AM to 10:44 AM on a 2.8-foot high tide at 10:14 AM. Tonight's fair window opens 10:27 PM to 11:27 PM on a 3.4-foot high at 10:57 PM. Tomorrow's morning window is 10:35 AM to 11:35 AM on a 2.7-foot high at 11:05 AM. The morning slot today is your better bet before any afternoon sea breeze develops. BHB is sheltered from offshore swell, so the 11-knot ESE wind is the main variable to watch. At 11 knots it is right at the edge, but most experienced BHB divers will find it workable during the peak of the tidal window.
