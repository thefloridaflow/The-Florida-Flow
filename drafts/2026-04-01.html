Conditions Headline

Seas are light to moderate across Florida's nearshore and inshore waters this morning, but a developing rain shower over the Atlantic is producing localized gusty winds. Buoy 41009 at the Space Coast (20 nm offshore) is reading 4.6 ft with an 8-second period, indicating typical spring swell, while the Treasure Coast buoy 41114 (6.5 nm offshore) shows 3.9 ft with a longer 11-second swell. South Florida bays are much calmer—buoy 41122 (23 nm offshore) near Fort Lauderdale and Miami reports just 2.6 ft with short 5-second periods, and water temperatures are warming, ranging 75–77°F across all regions.

The National Weather Service has issued an alert for a strong brief rain shower currently 13 nm east of Hillsboro Beach, moving west at 15 knots. This shower is capable of producing winds around 30 knots, heavier downpours, and locally higher waves. Waterspouts are possible with this activity. The shower is affecting coastal waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef, out 20 nm, and waters 20–60 nm offshore. Once it passes, conditions are expected to settle back to manageable levels through this evening.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) confirmed excellent diving conditions on March 28 with 70–80 ft visibility, 75°F water, no current, and 1 ft waves. Rainbow Reef in Key Largo has not yet posted today's report.

Blue Heron Bridge dive windows today are at 8:37 AM (2.7 ft, good window 8:07–9:07 AM) and 9:05 PM (3.0 ft, fair window 8:35–9:35 PM). Wind is running 14 knots from the ESE at the bridge, which will build slightly if the shower approaches.
