Conditions Headline

A strong rain shower is moving westward toward the coast this afternoon, capable of producing winds near 30 knots and locally higher waves. Offshore, buoy 41009 near Cocoa Beach (20 nm offshore) is reading 4.6 ft seas with an 8-second period, while buoy 41114 on the Treasure Coast (6.5 nm offshore) shows milder 3.9 ft seas with an 11-second period. The South Florida region shows the calmest conditions: buoy 41122 near Fort Lauderdale and Miami (23 nm offshore) is reporting 2.6 ft seas, while Key Largo sits at 3.0 ft (buoy 42095, 15 nm offshore). Wind is consistently ESE at 11–14 knots across all regions.

The National Weather Service issued a Marine Advisory for the Atlantic waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef extending 20–60 nm offshore. At 4:09 PM EDT, Doppler radar indicated a shower 13 nm east of Hillsboro Beach, moving west at 15 knots. Mariners can expect gusty winds to 30 knots, locally higher waves, lightning, and heavy downpours. Waterspouts are possible. The system is affecting coastal and offshore waters out to 60 nm. As it moves inland, conditions should ease this evening.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) confirmed excellent conditions on March 28, reporting 70–80 ft visibility, 75°F water, no current, and 1 ft waves.

Blue Heron Bridge has two dive windows today: the morning window peaks at 08:37 AM (2.7 ft) with a good rating, window 8:07 AM–9:07 AM. The evening window peaks at 09:05 PM (3.0 ft) with a fair rating, window 8:35 PM–9:35 PM. Both times should allow comfortable slack-water entries given protected tidal conditions.
