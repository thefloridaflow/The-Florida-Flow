Conditions Headline

Conditions are building throughout South Florida waters this afternoon due to a strong shower system moving west at 15 knots. The Space Coast is holding steady with 4.6 ft seas 20 nm offshore at buoy 41009, while the Treasure Coast sees 3.9 ft seas at buoy 41114 (6.5 nm offshore) and gentler conditions south of that. However, a line of heavy rain and wind capable of gusting to around 30 knots was located 13–14 nm east of the Southeast Florida coast as of late afternoon, with waterspout potential. This system is expected to impact coastal waters and near-shore areas from Jupiter Inlet through Ocean Reef.

The National Weather Service issued a Strong Shower Advisory for all Southeast Florida waters (Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef, 0–60 nm) this afternoon. Mariners can expect gusty winds to around 30 knots locally higher, rapidly building waves, lightning strikes, and heavy downpours. The system is moving westward, so conditions are expected to worsen before improving. Boaters and dive operators are advised to monitor radar and seek shelter immediately if the shower approaches.

Narcosis Dive Center (West Palm Beach) confirmed 70–80 ft visibility, 75 °F water, flat conditions, and no current as of March 28 — but those were calm conditions several days ago, not representative of today's active weather environment.

Blue Heron Bridge tide windows today are 08:37 AM (2.7 ft slack, good window 8:07–9:07 AM) and 09:05 PM (3.0 ft slack, fair window 8:35–9:35 PM). Morning is better for protected diving, but all water-sports decisions must account for the incoming shower system and boating advisory.

Daily Safety Tip
Waterspout & Lightning Awareness
A strong shower system with waterspout potential is moving across Southeast Florida this afternoon. If you see a rotating cloud formation over the water or funnel cloud, move away immediately and seek shelter on land. Lightning is the primary hazard — exit the water and all open areas the moment you hear thunder or see lightning. The shower was 13–14 nm offshore as of late afternoon and moving west at 15 knots, so conditions will deteriorate rapidly. Keep a VHF radio tuned to NOAA Weather Radio and monitor radar throughout the afternoon.
