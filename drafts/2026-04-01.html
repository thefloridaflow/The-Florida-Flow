Conditions Headline

A brief rain shower with winds gusting to around 30 knots is moving westward across Atlantic waters from Hillsboro Beach this afternoon. The Space Coast is seeing the most swell today—buoy 41009 (20 nm offshore) is reading 4.6 ft with an 8-second period—while the Treasure Coast sits calmer at 3.9 ft (buoy 41114, 6.5 nm offshore). South Florida and the Keys remain mild: seas 2.6–3.0 ft across the board, with water temperatures climbing into the mid-to-upper 70s. This is typical early-April variability: Atlantic swells favoring the north, inland waters protected and glassy.

The National Weather Service (AMZ630, issued this afternoon) confirms a strong shower capable of producing winds around 30 knots, locally higher waves, lightning, and heavy downpours. The system is moving west at 15 knots and was located 13 nm east of Hillsboro Beach at 4:09 PM EDT. Waterspouts are possible. Boaters in the forecast zone (Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef, out 20–60 nm) should seek safe harbor until the shower passes. The advisory covers coastal and offshore waters from Jupiter Inlet south to Ocean Reef.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) confirmed excellent conditions on March 28: 70–80 ft visibility, 75 °F water, flat seas, and no current. Those conditions reflect calm water before today's atmospheric disturbance.

Blue Heron Bridge tide windows today are favorable: morning high at 08:37 AM (2.7 ft) with a good dive window from 8:07–9:07 AM; evening high at 21:05 (2.95 ft) with a fair window from 8:35–9:35 PM. Morning entry is stronger; evening tide will be slack but slightly choppy due to the gusty wind event.
