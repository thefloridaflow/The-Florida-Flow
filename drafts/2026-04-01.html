Conditions Headline

A strong shower capable of producing winds to 30 knots and waterspouts is moving westward toward the coast, currently 13 nautical miles east of Hillsboro Beach. This system is affecting Southeast Florida coastal waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef, including areas 20–60 nm offshore. Ahead of the system, conditions remain mild: the Space Coast shows 4.6 ft seas with an 8-second period (buoy 41009, 20 nm offshore), while the Deerfield–Fort Lauderdale–Miami corridor is calm at 2.6 ft and 5-second period (buoy 41122, 23 nm offshore). Water temperatures are pleasant across the board, ranging from 75.7°F to 77.2°F.

The National Weather Service marine forecast (AMZ630, issued this morning) warns of a shower moving west at 15 knots, capable of producing gusty winds around 30 knots, locally higher waves, lightning, and heavy downpours. Waterspouts are possible. The alert covers all coastal and near-coastal waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef, as well as waters 20–60 nm offshore. Boaters are advised to seek safe harbor immediately until the system passes.

Narcosis Dive in West Palm Beach confirmed outstanding dive conditions on March 28: 70–80 ft visibility, 75°F water, no current, and 1 ft waves. Rainbow Reef in Key Largo was expected to post a report this morning but details were not yet available as of publication.

Blue Heron Bridge presents a good morning tide window this morning at 8:37 AM (2.7 ft range) with a window from 8:07 AM to 9:07 AM, and a fair evening window at 9:05 PM (3.0 ft range) from 8:35 PM to 9:35 PM. Morning winds are 14.0 kt ESE and water is 76.3°F. Verify current shower status with your operator before committing to a dive.
