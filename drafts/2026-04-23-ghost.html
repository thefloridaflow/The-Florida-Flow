The Briefing

A steady easing trend brings the best conditions of the week by afternoon

Yesterday was the gusty day, with buoy 41009 logging wind near 10 kt and offshore seas running 3.6 ft on an 8-second period off the Space Coast. The Treasure Coast buoy (41114, 6.5 nm offshore) is showing 3.3 ft with an 8-second period and water at a comfortable 76.5°F. Those longer 8-second intervals have kept rip risk somewhat elevated on exposed stretches, but NWS is already calling for seas to settle through today and into Friday.

The NWS marine forecast for Jupiter Inlet to Deerfield Beach calls for E winds 10-15 kt today, dropping to around 10 kt tonight, then SE around 10 kt on Friday with seas near 2 ft. The Gulf Stream's west wall is sitting just 3 nm east-northeast of Lake Worth, which keeps the current-against-wind chop scenario in play for anyone heading well offshore this morning. That said, the inshore and nearshore picture is measurably calmer than Wednesday, and this afternoon's Blue Heron Bridge tide window at 1:35 PM is rated optimal with a 2.4 ft high. Wind at the bridge is currently 15 kt out of the east, which is at the upper edge for BHB comfort. If it drops toward 10-12 kt by afternoon, as the forecast suggests, the window will be excellent.

Narcosis Dive reported 60 ft visibility out of West Palm Beach on Sunday, April 20, with a Caribbean reef shark and a Goliath grouper in the mix. That report came before this week's breezy easterly spell, so real-time visibility may be somewhat reduced today with E 15 kt onshore wind. Expect 10-30 ft nearshore, closer to 20-40 ft if you can get offshore of the surface chop. The Short 4-5 second wave period (from the NWS wave detail: E 3 ft at 5 seconds) means the bottom is less stirred than the surface looks, so vis at depth is likely better than topside conditions suggest.

Activity planner

Thursday · 6 activities
Scuba Diving Caution

E 15 kt at the surface this morning keeps nearshore viz in the 10-25 ft range with 8-second swells off the Space Coast. Seas of 3.0-3.6 ft are on the choppy end but manageable for most dive boats. Afternoon should improve as wind drops toward 10 kt. Short 5-second period per NWS means bottom sediment is less stirred than surface looks. BHB afternoon window is viable if wind backs off. Verify with your operator before leaving the dock.

Surfing Choppy

E 15 kt onshore wind is creating surface chop that degrades wave shape. Buoy 41009 shows 3.6 ft at 8 seconds off the Space Coast, and 41114 shows 3.3 ft at 8 seconds near the Treasure Coast. There is rideable swell in the water but it is wind-affected. Expect bumpy, crumbly conditions this morning. Conditions will not improve significantly until winds drop below 12 kt, likely this afternoon. Verify with your local break report.

Kayak / SUP Not recommended

E 15 kt is above the 10 kt threshold for safe open-water paddling, and seas of 3.0-3.6 ft make exposed coastal water risky for sit-on-tops and paddleboards. Protected inlets, rivers, and mangrove channels are suitable alternatives today. Conditions ease toward the weekend. Verify local conditions before launching.

Boating / Fishing Good

Larger offshore vessels can handle today's 2-3 ft nearshore, 3-3.6 ft offshore conditions with moderate chop. Inshore fishing on the ICW and inlets should be comfortable. No active advisory in place. Gulf Stream is 3 nm east of Lake Worth, adding some current-against-wind chop for anyone running offshore this morning. Wind drops tonight to around 10 kt. Verify with your captain before departure.

Beach Good

Partly cloudy, UV index 7 (High). Water sits at 76.5-77°F, comfortable for swimming. Seas of 2-3 ft are normal for this coast. The 8-second period warrants caution for rip currents on exposed beaches. Swim near a lifeguard stand. Check posted flags on arrival. Afternoon offers lower wind and pleasant conditions for beach time.

Gulf Stream Passage Caution

The Gulf Stream's west wall is 3 nm east-northeast of Lake Worth and 10 nm northeast of Port Everglades as of April 20. NWS notes easterly winds 10-15 kt with higher gusts crossing the Stream this morning, which creates short steep seas where the current runs against the wind. Conditions ease this afternoon. Exercise caution on Stream crossings until wind drops to around 10 kt. Verify with your captain.
