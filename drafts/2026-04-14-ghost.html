Safety Tip

Waterspout Awareness: Know When to Get Off the Water.

Today's Special Marine Warning was triggered by radar-detected waterspouts near Hillsboro Beach moving south at 20 knots. Waterspouts can develop and intensify in minutes with little visual warning from the surface, and the associated wind gusts can reach 50 knots within a very small area. If you see a rotating column of air or water over the ocean, treat it as a tornado and get to shore immediately.

Before any offshore trip, check the NWS marine forecast and active warnings at weather.gov. A Special Marine Warning is not a routine advisory. It means leave now or stay in.
