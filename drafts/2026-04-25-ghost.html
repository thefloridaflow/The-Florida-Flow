The Briefing

A gentle Saturday on the water, with drizzle the only asterisk

Conditions along the Southeast Florida coast are comfortable and consistent this morning. Buoys show seas of 2.0 to 2.6 ft at long periods of 11 seconds offshore (Space Coast and Treasure Coast) and a shorter 4-second chop further south off Deerfield and Miami at 2.0 ft. The NWS forecast calls for 5 to 10 kt east-to-southeast winds through the day, seas around 2 ft, and light chop on the Intracoastal. An upper-level disturbance may bring scattered showers and isolated thunderstorms through the weekend, so check radar before heading out, but the sea state itself is not a concern.

The morning window at 3:29 to 4:29 AM is fair but pre-dawn. Activity planner

Saturday · 6 activities
Scuba Diving Go

Seas 2.0 to 2.6 ft with SE winds 7 to 10 kt. Operator reports confirm 50 to 60 ft visibility offshore Palm Beach yesterday. The afternoon BHB window (3:58 to 4:58 PM) is the top pick for shore diving. Scattered showers are possible, so watch radar. Verify with your operator before heading out.

Surfing Fair

Seas around 2 ft with a 4-second period from the south offshore. Short period and onshore SE wind make for weak, crumbly surf. Space Coast shows 11-second period which is better quality but only 2.3 ft. Manageable for beginners; experienced surfers may find it soft. Verify with your local surf shop.

Kayak / SUP Go

Intracoastal winds at 7 kt SE are ideal for flatwater paddling. Intracoastal is light chop per NWS. Avoid open ocean in 2 ft seas unless experienced. Morning hours will be calmest before any afternoon shower activity. Confirm launch conditions locally before heading out.

Boating / Fishing Go

2 ft seas and 5 to 10 kt winds are comfortable for most boats. The Gulf Stream wall sits as close as 3 nautical miles off Lake Worth, making it an accessible troll day for pelagic species. Port Everglades current is a light 0.29 kt north. No SCA in effect. Watch for afternoon isolated showers. Verify with your captain.

Beach Go

Water temps 76 to 79°F coast-wide. Seas are mild. UV Index 9 (Very High) -- apply SPF 50 and reapply every hour. Drizzle is possible as an upper-level disturbance moves through. Swim near a lifeguard. Check posted flags on arrival.

Gulf Stream Go

The west wall of the Gulf Stream is unusually close, just 3 nautical miles off Lake Worth and 6 nautical miles off Jupiter Inlet as of April 20. Seas are 2 ft with light winds, making this a good window for Stream fishing. No Gulf Stream hazards active per NWS. Verify with your captain before departure.

Weekend beach report

Casual beachgoers · plain English

Water temperatures are running 76 to 79°F coast-wide -- warm and comfortable for swimming. Seas are mild at 2 ft with light southeast winds under 10 kt. The surf is gentle, not the kind of day that will push you around in the water.

Rip current risk is low to moderate. Waves from the south at short periods (4 to 5 seconds) are not stacking on the beach in a way that typically drives strong rips, though onshore SE flow and the 11-second swell noted at the Space Coast buoys can produce localized pull. Swim near a lifeguard stand if in doubt.

Expect yellow flags at most beaches today (estimate from NOAA data -- check posted flags on arrival). Morning is the best window before any afternoon drizzle arrives. UV Index is 9 (Very High) -- reef-safe sunscreen, hat, and shade breaks are a must by late morning.
