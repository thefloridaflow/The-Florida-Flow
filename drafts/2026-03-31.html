Conditions Headline

A strong shower is moving westward toward the Florida coast this afternoon with locally gusty winds to 30 knots and the potential for waterspout development. Space Coast buoy 41009 (20 nm offshore) is reading 6.6 ft seas with 10-second period under 15.6 kt ESE winds — the largest seas of the day. Northward, Jupiter (buoy 41114, 12 nm offshore) shows 5.2 ft with 9-second period and water at 75.7°F. Southern regions show moderate building: Deerfield to Miami (buoy 41122, 23 nm offshore) reading 4.9 ft with 6-second period, water 77.2°F, while Upper Keys (buoy 42095, 15 nm offshore) remain tamest at 3.9 ft.

NWS issued a strong shower warning at 4:09 PM EDT affecting coastal waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef FL out 20 nm, and waters 20–60 nm from Deerfield Beach to Ocean Reef. Mariners can expect gusty winds around 30 knots (locally higher), locally higher waves, lightning, and heavy downpours. Waterspouts are possible. The system is moving west at 15 knots. After passage, conditions are expected to moderate overnight and into Wednesday morning.

Narcosis Dive Center (West Palm Beach) confirmed conditions on March 28: 70–80 ft visibility, 75°F water, no current, 1 ft waves. Rainbow Reef in Key Largo did not post a report by this morning's update window.

Blue Heron Bridge tide windows today: 07:57 AM high (2.7 ft) with fair window 7:27–8:27 AM, and 08:22 PM high (2.9 ft) with fair window 7:52–8:52 PM. Both windows favor early morning or after the shower passes.
