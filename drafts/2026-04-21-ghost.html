The Briefing

A cold front pushed through overnight — offshore is off the table through Wednesday

A Small Craft Advisory is in effect from Jupiter Inlet to Deerfield Beach out to 60 nautical miles, running through Wednesday morning. The cold front that moved across local waters last night drove northeast winds up sharply — buoy LKWF1 at Blue Heron Bridge is reading 24.1 kt NE as of this morning, and 41009 off the Space Coast (20 nm offshore) is reporting seas of 6.9 ft at a 9-second period. In the Gulf Stream, NWS expects seas of 8 to 12 ft with northeast winds of 20 to 30 kt through Tuesday night. There is no case for an offshore trip today.

Closer to shore, things are less extreme but still demanding. The 6.5 nm Treasure Coast buoy (41114) shows 5.9 ft seas at an 8-second period and 78.1°F water — rough enough to cancel most dive charters. Farther south, the 23 nm offshore buoy 41122 shows 3.9 ft at a 6-second period, which is elevated but manageable for larger vessels south of Lake Worth. Blue Heron Bridge is the clearest opportunity today: wind at 24.1 kt NE is high, but BHB is a sheltered inshore site where tidal flow matters far more than wind. The afternoon slack window around 12:59 PM (12:29–1:29 PM) is the call if you want to get wet today. Visibility at BHB will be tidal-limited, roughly 5 to 15 ft given current NE wind direction.

The good news: this front is moving fast. Tonight's winds ease to 11-15 kt by midnight, and Wednesday through the weekend looks progressively lighter — partly cloudy Wednesday with gusts to 20 kt, dropping to 11-13 kt gusts by Friday and Saturday. If you have the flexibility to wait 48 hours, conditions improve substantially. Thursday carries the next meaningful rain event (10.6 mm forecast), but winds will be mild at 11 kt max.

Activity planner

Tuesday · 6 activities
Scuba Diving BHB Only

Offshore trips are not viable today. The SCA covers Jupiter to Deerfield out 60 nm, with Space Coast seas at 6.9 ft and Treasure Coast at 5.9 ft. Blue Heron Bridge is the only realistic option: the afternoon tide window at 12:59 PM (12:29–1:29 PM) is optimal. Vis will be tidal, roughly 5–15 ft given 24.1 kt NE wind at LKWF1. Verify conditions with your operator before heading out.

Surfing Choppy

There is swell: Space Coast is showing 6.9 ft at a 9-second period. However, 15–24 kt onshore NE winds will blow out surface conditions. Surf will be messy and difficult to read. Experienced surfers may find rideable waves early in the morning before wind builds further, but conditions are not clean. Check local spots and verify with your operator.

Kayak / SUP Not Advised

24.1 kt NE winds at LKWF1 exceed the safe threshold for open-water kayaking or SUP by a wide margin. Exposed coastal paddling is not safe today. Protected flat water (ICW, lagoon, mangrove channels) may be possible for experienced paddlers, but check local wind sheltering carefully. Verify conditions with your guide or outfitter before going out.

Boating / Fishing Offshore: No

An active Small Craft Advisory is in effect through Wednesday morning for all offshore waters from Jupiter to Deerfield out 60 nm. The NWS cites NE winds 20–30 kt and seas building to 8–12 ft in the Gulf Stream. Offshore fishing is inadvisable today. Inshore fishing on the ICW, back bays, or nearshore reefs (calm-water vessels only) may still be viable. Verify with your captain before departing.

Beach Caution

The beach is accessible but conditions are breezy and surf is rough. Water is a comfortable 77–79°F. Expect red or double-red flags at many locations given the SCA and elevated seas. Rip current risk is HIGH with 5.9–6.9 ft seas at 8–9-second periods and strong onshore NE wind. Do not swim in unguarded areas today. Verify posted flags on arrival.

Gulf Stream Do Not Go

NWS has issued a Gulf Stream hazard specifically citing NE winds of 20–30 kt and seas building to 8–12 ft through Tuesday night. The Gulf Stream west wall sits 3 nm east of Lake Worth and 7 nm south of Port Everglades. Do not attempt Gulf Stream crossings or blue-water fishing trips today. Verify with your captain before departing.

Beach report

Casual beachgoers · Plain English

Water temperatures are sitting at 77 to 79°F — warm and comfortable to the touch. The surf is rough today, not just choppy. A cold front crossed overnight, and strong northeast winds are pushing big waves into the beach.

Rip current risk is HIGH. Seas of 6 to 7 feet at the Space Coast and nearly 6 feet at the Treasure Coast, combined with a strong onshore northeast wind and 8 to 9-second wave periods, create dangerous rip current conditions. Swim near a lifeguard stand. If you get caught in a rip, swim parallel to shore until you are clear, then swim back in at an angle.

Expect red flags — or double-red flags at some locations. Walking the beach, building sand castles, and wading ankle-deep are all fine. Getting in past your knees is not a good idea today.

The afternoon may be slightly more pleasant for a stroll as skies are forecast to be partly cloudy. UV is Very High (index 9) so sunscreen is essential even with cloud cover.

Conditions change — always check posted flags and swim near a lifeguard.
