Safety Tip

Waterspouts form fast and move faster than you think. Today's Special Marine Warning was triggered by radar-indicated showers moving south at 20 knots along the Palm Beach and Broward coast. Waterspouts can form within minutes from developing convective cells, and the gusts immediately surrounding them can reach 50 knots even when the broader forecast looks light. If you see a rotating column of water or funnel cloud while on the water, turn away from it immediately and head to shore or a protected anchorage. Do not attempt to motor around a waterspout -- their track is unpredictable. Always check for active marine warnings at tidesandcurrents.noaa.gov or on VHF weather radio before leaving the dock.
