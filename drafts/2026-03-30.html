Conditions Headline

Seas are building on the Space Coast this morning, with buoy 41009 (20 nm offshore) reading 8.5 ft in a 10-second period and 15.6-knot easterly winds. Offshore, conditions are rougher—Jupiter's buoy 41114 (12 nm offshore) shows 8.2 ft seas with an 11-second period and water at 75.7°F. South of Deerfield, seas drop to 6.2 ft (buoy 41122, 23 nm offshore), and the Keys are calmer at 3.3 ft (buoy 42095, 15 nm offshore). Inshore winds are elevated: LKWF1 (Blue Heron Bridge / Palm Beach) is reading 22.9 knots easterly with water at 76.5°F.

NWS Marine Forecast (AMZ630, issued this afternoon) describes a strong shower capable of producing winds to around 30 knots, located 13 nm east of Hillsboro Beach and moving west at 15 knots. The affected areas include coastal waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef out to 60 nm. Mariners can expect gusty winds, locally higher waves, lightning strikes, heavy downpours, and possible waterspouts. Boaters should seek safe harbor immediately until the shower passes.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) confirmed on March 28 that inshore conditions were excellent: 70–80 ft visibility, 75°F water, no current, and 1 ft waves. Rainbow Reef (Key Largo) reported on March 29 at 02:53 PM that visibility was 2–3 feet inshore and 40 feet at depth, with easterly wind at 15 knots.

Blue Heron Bridge tide windows today: morning window 6:43–7:43 AM (high tide 2.7 ft at 07:13 AM, fair conditions); evening window 7:06–8:06 PM (high tide 2.8 ft at 19:36, fair conditions). Wind at 22.9 knots easterly will create chop inshore throughout the day—verify tide strategy with your operator.
