⚠️ STRONG SHOWER ALERT: A shower capable of producing winds to 30 knots is moving west across Atlantic waters from Jupiter to Ocean Reef out 60 nm. Seek safe harbor immediately. Waterspouts possible.
Conditions Headline

Seas are building across the northern reaches this morning with Space Coast running 8.5 ft and 10-second periods offshore, while protected nearshore zones from Deerfield south remain calmer at 6.2 ft. The Upper Keys stay in pocket-sized 3 ft conditions. Easterly winds are steady at 15–20 knots across all zones, but a strong offshore shower capable of producing gusts to 30 knots is moving westward and will impact waters from Jupiter to Ocean Reef out 60 nm this afternoon and evening.

The National Weather Service (AMZ630, issued 4:09 PM EDT yesterday) has issued a strong shower alert affecting coastal and offshore waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef extending 60 nm. Mariners can expect locally higher waves, lightning, heavy downpours, and waterspouts are possible. This system is moving west at 15 knots and boaters should seek safe harbor until it passes.

Narcosis Dive in West Palm Beach confirmed excellent conditions on March 28 with 70–80 ft visibility, 75°F water, and negligible current — but that report is now two days old. Rainbow Reef in Key Largo reported marginal conditions on March 29 with only 2–3 ft visibility beyond 40 ft, suggesting typical Upper Keys chop and reduced sight lines yesterday afternoon.

Blue Heron Bridge dive windows today are tight but usable: morning slack at 7:13 AM (2.7 ft) with a 1-hour window (6:43–7:43 AM), and evening slack at 7:36 PM (2.8 ft) with window 7:06–8:06 PM. Both qualify as fair conditions given the 20 kt E wind, which may create surface chop near the bridge entrance.
