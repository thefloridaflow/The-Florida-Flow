⚠️ STRONG SHOWER MOVING WEST: Winds gusting to 30 knots possible this afternoon across Jupiter to Deerfield coastal waters. Waterspouts possible. Boaters should seek shelter immediately.
Conditions Headline

Space Coast and Jupiter remain rough with 8–9 foot seas driven by 17–18 knot easterly winds, while South Florida's protected waters show the typical 6-foot offshore swell and 75–77°F water temperatures. A strong shower capable of 30-knot gusts is tracking west toward the coast this afternoon from 13 nm east of Hillsboro Beach, affecting Jupiter through Deerfield waters out to 60 nm. This is the main story: conditions are deteriorating rapidly through the afternoon.

National Weather Service issued a strong shower warning for AMZ630 (coastal and offshore waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef) at approximately 4:09 PM EDT. Mariners should expect gusty winds around 30 knots locally higher, locally higher waves, lightning strikes, and heavy downpours. Waterspouts are possible. Boaters are advised to seek safe harbor immediately until the storm passes. The shower was moving west at 15 knots, so impact could extend into evening hours depending on track.

Narcosis Dive in West Palm Beach reported excellent conditions on March 28, with 70–80 foot visibility, 75°F water, no current, and 1-foot waves—a solid recent benchmark for protected inland areas. Rainbow Reef in Key Largo (March 29) reported 2–3 foot visibility at 40 feet with no current and 15-knot winds, indicating variable conditions in the Upper Keys.

Blue Heron Bridge presents two windows today: 6:43–7:43 AM (high 2.7 ft, fair rating) and 7:06–8:06 PM (high 2.8 ft, fair rating). Early morning is your safest bet to avoid the incoming shower; evening may coincide with or immediately follow the severe weather window.
