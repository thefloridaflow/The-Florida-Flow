Conditions Headline

South Florida waters are running calm to moderate this morning, with the Space Coast showing the most swell at 4.6 ft (buoy 41009, 20 nm offshore) and the Treasure Coast at 4.6 ft (buoy 41114, 6.5 nm offshore), while inshore areas and the Keys stay smaller. A brief but significant rain shower with winds gusting to 30 knots is moving west across the Atlantic, currently 13 nm east of Hillsboro Beach and expected to impact coastal and offshore waters throughout today. Water temperatures are warming steadily, ranging from 76–77°F across all regions.

NOAA Marine Forecast AMZ630 (issued this morning, 4:09 PM EDT) reports a strong shower capable of producing winds around 30 knots, with locally higher waves, lightning strikes, and heavy downpours possible. The system is moving west at 15 knots and will affect coastal waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef out 20 nm, as well as offshore waters from 20 to 60 nm. Waterspouts are possible—mariners should remain on alert. Conditions should improve once the shower passes.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) reported excellent conditions on April 1st, with 50–60 ft visibility, 77°F water, and a trickle northbound current. Divers confirmed sightings of hawksbill and green morays, spotted morays, tropical fish, and healthy corals and sponges. Rainbow Reef (Key Largo) has an overnight report pending.

Blue Heron Bridge dive windows today: 09:16 AM (2.6 ft) offers a good window from 8:46 AM to 9:46 AM; 09:45 PM (3.0 ft) is fair from 9:15 PM to 10:15 PM. Both windows remain protected from today's swell, though the afternoon and evening shower will bring wind and reduced visibility—verify timing and conditions with your dive operator before heading out.
