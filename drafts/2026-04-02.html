Conditions Headline

Seas across the region are modest and manageable this morning. Space Coast buoy 41009 (20 nm offshore) is reading 4.6 ft with a 9-second period, while the Gold Coast and Keys are all running 3–4 ft offshore. Water temps are holding steady in the mid-to-upper 70s from Treasure Coast south. The big change: a shower with gusty winds developed east of Hillsboro Beach this afternoon and is moving westward at 15 knots, capable of producing winds near 30 knots and waterspouts. This system is primarily affecting waters 13–20 nm offshore and beyond; inshore areas are less affected, but boaters should stay alert.

NWS marine forecast (AMZ630, issued this morning) warns of a shower capable of producing winds to around 30 knots, locally higher waves, lightning, and heavy downpours in coastal and offshore waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef. The system is moving west at 15 knots. Waterspouts are possible. Conditions should begin to improve as the shower passes, likely within the next few hours.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) reported excellent conditions on April 1: 50–60 ft visibility, 77°F water, a trickle north current, and 3–4 ft waves. Divers saw hawksbill and green turtles, spotted morays, tropical fish, and healthy corals and sponges.

Blue Heron Bridge dive windows today: 9:16 AM (2.6 ft, good window 8:46–9:46 AM) and 9:45 PM (3.0 ft, fair window 9:15–10:15 PM). Winds are easterly at 14 knots; tidal flow is manageable at both slots. Verify with your dive operator before heading to BHB.
