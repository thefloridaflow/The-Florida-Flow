Conditions Headline

A strong shower is moving west across Atlantic waters off the SE Florida coast this afternoon, with seas running 3–4.6 feet offshore and winds expected to gust to 30 knots. Buoy 41009 near Cocoa Beach (20 nm offshore) is reading 4.6 feet with a 9-second period and 11.7-knot easterly wind. Buoy 41114 off the Treasure Coast (6.5 nm offshore) shows 4.3 feet and 5-second period. South of Deerfield, buoy 41122 (23 nm offshore) is running 3.9 feet. The Upper Keys remain calmer at 3.0 feet on buoy 42095 (15 nm offshore). Nearshore conditions will be notably smaller than these offshore readings.

NWS marine forecast for AMZ630 issued this morning warns of a shower capable of producing winds around 30 knots, with locally higher waves, lightning, and heavy downpours. This activity is located 13 nm east of Hillsboro Beach, moving west at 15 knots. Waterspouts are possible. The forecast zone covers coastal waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef out 20 nm, plus offshore waters 20–60 nm. Conditions are expected to improve as the shower passes, but mariners are advised to remain alert.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) confirmed excellent conditions yesterday with 50–60 feet visibility, 77°F water, light northerly current, 3–4 foot waves, and strong tropical fish, sea turtles, moray eels, and coral sightings. Rainbow Reef in Key Largo did not report overnight conditions.

Blue Heron Bridge tide windows today: High tide at 9:16 AM (2.6 ft) with a good window 8:46–9:46 AM, and High at 9:45 PM (3.0 ft) with a fair window 9:15–10:15 PM. East winds of 14 knots from buoy LKWF1 may create choppy conditions on the bridge during both windows — check with your captain before entering.
