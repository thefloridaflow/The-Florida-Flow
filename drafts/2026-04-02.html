Conditions Headline

Seas remain moderate across South Florida with a temporary squall moving into Atlantic waters offshore this afternoon. Space Coast buoy 41009 (20 nm offshore) is reporting 4.6 ft seas with an 8-second period — clean swell energy. Treasure Coast and Gold Coast buoys show smaller 4.6 ft and 3.9 ft respectively, both with shorter 5-second periods typical of local wind chop. Upper Keys are calm at 2.6 ft. Water temperatures are uniformly warm: 76–77°F across all regions.

NWS zone AMZ630 issued a strong shower warning this morning for coastal and offshore Atlantic waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef, from shore out to 60 nm. A brief but intense rain shower capable of 30-knot winds was located 13–14 nm east of Pompano/Lighthouse Point as of 4:09 PM EDT, moving west at 15 knots. Waterspouts are possible with this activity. The system is expected to pass quickly, but conditions in affected zones should be monitored closely. Inshore areas and the Gulf side remain unaffected.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) confirmed excellent conditions on April 1, with 50–60 ft visibility, 77°F water, and light trickle-north current. Divers reported hawksbill and green turtles, spotted moray, healthy corals, and sponges. Rainbow Reef (Key Largo) has not posted a trip report in the last 48 hours; no confirmed sightings from them today.

Blue Heron Bridge is running two windows today: morning high tide at 09:16 AM (2.6 ft current, good window 8:46–9:46 AM) and evening high at 09:45 PM (3.0 ft current, fair window 9:15–10:15 PM). East winds at 17.1 kt are building slightly and may create surface turbulence, especially during the evening window.

Waterspout Alert & Offshore Shower Safety
A strong shower warning is active in Atlantic waters 20+ nm offshore with potential for waterspouts, 30-knot winds, and lightning. If you are boating offshore, monitor this system constantly and seek safe harbor immediately if conditions deteriorate or a waterspout warning is issued. Even brief squalls can create dangerous wind shear and reduced visibility. Inshore boaters and divers should stay on alert for sudden wind gusts and rain; these systems move quickly but pack intensity. Verify with your captain or Coast Guard forecast before leaving the inlet.
