Conditions Headline

Moderate seas are running across South and Treasure Coasts today, with buoy 41009 (20 nm offshore) at the Space Coast reading 4.9 ft and 8-second period, while inshore areas show smaller building conditions. A brief but intense rain shower is moving westward toward the coast this afternoon, capable of producing gusts near 30 knots and possible waterspouts — mariners from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef should remain alert.

The National Weather Service (AMZ630, issued this morning) warns of a strong shower approaching coastal waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef and extending 20–60 nm offshore. Winds across the region are running 13–15 knots from the east with building seas. Current through Port Everglades is minimal at 0.58 knots north. Water temperatures are holding in the mid-to-upper 70s across all zones.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) reported strong conditions on April 1st: 50–60 ft visibility, 77°F water, light northbound current, 3–4 ft waves, and active marine life including a hawksbill turtle, green and spotted morays, tropical fish, and healthy corals and sponges. Rainbow Reef (Key Largo) posted overnight but did not include detailed conditions in this morning's report.

Blue Heron Bridge tidal windows today: 09:16 AM high tide (2.6 ft) — good window 8:46–9:46 AM; 09:45 PM high tide (3.0 ft) — fair window 9:15–10:15 PM. Morning window is the stronger choice. Light easterly wind at 15 knots will be manageable for most paddlers.

Watch for Waterspouts — Brief Storm Approach
A brief but intense rain shower capable of producing 30-knot gusts is moving toward the coast today. The National Weather Service warns that waterspouts are possible with this activity. If you see a rotating cloud funnel over the water, move to safe harbor immediately. Keep one eye on the horizon, especially from mid-afternoon onward.
