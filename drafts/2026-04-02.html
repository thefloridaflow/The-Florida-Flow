Conditions Headline

Easterly winds at 13–15 knots are holding modest swell across South and Central Florida this morning. Buoy 41009 off the Space Coast (20 nm offshore) is reading 4.9 ft with an 8-second period; buoy 41114 near the Treasure Coast (6.5 nm offshore) shows 4.6 ft seas with a 5-second period; and buoy 41122 south of Fort Lauderdale (23 nm offshore) shows 4.3 ft with a 6-second period. The Keys remain protected at 3.0 ft. Water temperatures are holding steady in the 76–77°F range across most regions, with slightly warmer conditions near Deerfield (77.2°F).

The National Weather Service issued a marine forecast for coastal waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef noting a strong brief rain shower moving west across Atlantic waters at 15 knots as of 4:09 PM EDT. This activity is capable of producing winds around 30 knots, locally higher waves, heavy downpours, and lightning. Waterspouts are possible. Beyond this shower activity, winds remain light and conditions are expected to remain relatively stable through tonight.

Narcosis Dive reported from West Palm Beach yesterday with exceptional visibility of 50–60 feet, water at 77°F, and a trickle northerly current. Divers confirmed sightings of tropical fish, a hawksbill turtle, green and spotted moray eels, corals, and sponges. Rainbow Reef in Key Largo did not file an overnight report today.

Blue Heron Bridge tide windows today favor morning diving: a good window at 8:46–9:46 AM corresponding to high tide at 2.6 ft, and a fair evening window at 9:15–10:15 PM at 3.0 ft. Winds remain manageable at 15 knots east. Verify entry and exit timing with your operator.
