Conditions Headline

A brief rain shower capable of producing winds around 30 knots is moving west across the Atlantic waters east of Broward County. Seas are running 4–5 feet on offshore buoys (20+ nm out), with water temperatures ranging 76–77°F. The Space Coast is seeing slightly larger swells (4.9 ft, 8-second period at buoy 41009, 20 nm offshore), while the Dade County region is calmer at 4.3 ft (buoy 41122, 23 nm offshore). The Upper Keys near Key Largo are the calmest zone, with 3.0 ft seas reported.

NWS AMZ630 issued a marine advisory this afternoon for all coastal waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef, extending 0–60 nm offshore. Expect gusty winds to around 30 knots locally, heavy downpours, lightning, and possible waterspouts. The shower was located 13 nm east of Hillsboro Beach at 4:09 PM EDT and moving west at 15 knots. Mariners are advised to seek safe harbor immediately until the system passes.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) reported excellent conditions on April 1st: 50–60 ft visibility, 77°F water, trickle-north current, 3–4 ft waves on the surface, and active marine life including a hawksbill turtle, green and spotted morays, tropical fish, corals, and sponges. Rainbow Reef in Key Largo has no overnight report posted yet.

Blue Heron Bridge dive windows today (Lake Worth): 09:16 AM high tide (2.6 ft) with a good window from 8:46–9:46 AM, and 09:45 PM high tide (3.0 ft) with a fair window from 9:15–10:15 PM. Northeast winds at 15 kt from the buoy this morning.

Daily Safety Tip
Waterspout Awareness in Rough Seas
A brief rain shower with 30-knot gusts is moving west across the Atlantic today, and waterspouts are possible. Waterspouts can form rapidly over warm water during thunderstorms. If you see a rotating cloud funnel or dark vortex approaching, move perpendicular to it immediately and seek shelter. Do not enter the water if lightning is visible or thunder is audible. Check with your captain and stay alert to NWS marine warnings (AMZ630) throughout the afternoon and evening.
