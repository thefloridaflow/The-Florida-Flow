MARINE ADVISORY: A brief rain shower capable of producing winds near 30 knots is moving west toward the Treasure and Deerfield coasts. Waterspouts are possible. Boaters should seek safe harbor immediately. (NWS AMZ630, issued 4:09 PM EDT)
Conditions Headline

Seas are building across the outer coast this afternoon. Space Coast waters are running 4.9 ft at buoy 41009 (20 nm offshore), while the Treasure Coast shows 4.6 ft at buoy 41114 (6.5 nm offshore). A strong rain shower with gusty winds is approaching the South Florida coast and expected to produce winds near 30 knots as it moves west through the afternoon and evening.

The National Weather Service issued a marine advisory for coastal and nearshore waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef, out to 60 nm offshore. Conditions affected include all waters from Jupiter Inlet to Deerfield Beach and from Deerfield Beach to Ocean Reef. A shower with heavy rain, lightning, and locally higher waves is expected to move through the area. Waterspouts are possible during this activity. Mariners in the forecast zone should remain alert and seek safe harbor immediately if the shower approaches. Check with your captain before heading out.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) reported 50–60 ft visibility, 77 °F water, and a trickle northbound current on April 1. Divers observed a hawksbill turtle, green and spotted morays, tropical fish, and corals.

Blue Heron Bridge today presents a good dive window at 9:16 AM (2.6 ft slack, 8:46–9:46 AM) and a fair window at 9:45 PM (3.0 ft slack, 9:15–10:15 PM). East winds at 15 knots are pushing through the area; verify conditions with your operator before entering.
