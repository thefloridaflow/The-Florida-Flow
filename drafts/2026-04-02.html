Conditions Headline

A brief but strong rain shower is moving westward toward the Treasure Coast and Southeast Florida coastline this evening, with gusts capable of reaching 30 knots. Offshore, buoy 41009 (Space Coast, 20 nm out) is reading 4.9 ft seas with 8-second periods and 13.6 kt easterly wind — nearshore conditions will be smaller. Inshore areas from West Palm Beach to Key Largo remain mild: buoy 41114 (Treasure Coast, 6.5 nm offshore) shows 4.6 ft seas with a 5-second period, and water temps hold steady in the mid-70s across all regions. The shower activity is localized and moving quickly inland.

NWS Miami (AMZ630, issued 4:09 PM EDT) warns of a strong shower with winds around 30 knots, locally higher waves, lightning, and heavy downpours affecting coastal and offshore waters from Jupiter Inlet to Ocean Reef out 20 nm, and waters 20–60 nm from shore in the same zone. Waterspouts are possible. The shower was 13 nm east of Hillsboro Beach at time of issuance, moving west at 15 knots. Boaters should seek safe harbor immediately.

Narcosis Dive (West Palm Beach) confirmed excellent conditions yesterday: visibility 50–60 ft, water 77 °F, negligible northward current, 3–4 ft waves, and abundant tropical fish, a hawksbill turtle, two moray eels, and healthy corals and sponges.

Blue Heron Bridge windows today are tight but diveable. High tide at 09:16 AM (2.6 ft slack) with a good dive window from 8:46–9:46 AM. Evening slack at 9:45 PM (3.0 ft) offers a fair window from 9:15–10:15 PM. Wind remains 15.0 kt easterly, so verify conditions with your dive operator before heading out — the evening window may be affected by the approaching shower.

Waterspout Risk & Lightning Safety — Strong Shower Overhead
A strong rain shower capable of 30-knot gusts is moving toward South Florida this evening, with waterspouts possible over coastal and offshore waters. If you are on the water or beach, monitor radar and weather alerts closely. Do not enter or re-enter the water if you hear thunder or see lightning — even distant flashes mean lightning is a threat within 10 miles. If caught in the shower, seek immediate shelter inland or at a marina. The system is moving quickly westward at 15 knots, so conditions should clear within 2–4 hours.
